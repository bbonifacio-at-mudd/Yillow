Data Cleaning Process

Cleaning data is an essential process in any data science project. It ensures data quality and improves the reliability of our analysis. Here's a look at how we cleaned our dataset for the Yillow Project.

Removing Duplicates

First and foremost, we made sure there were no duplicate entries in our dataset. Duplicate records can skew the analysis and provide inaccurate results.

Handling Missing Values

Next, we dealt with missing values. Wherever data was missing, we assigned a 'NoValue' tag. This way, we retain the record while acknowledging the absence of certain information.

Outlier Detection and Removal

Outliers can often skew the results of an analysis. We used box-and-whisker plots to visualize the range of our data and identify these outliers.

Ensuring Data Consistency

Lastly, we ensured data consistency. It's vital that the same type of data is represented consistently across the dataset. For example, if living area is represented in square feet, we made sure it's not accidentally entered as square meters in some instances.

Conclusion

With these steps, we made sure our data was clean and ready for further analysis. This process enhances the reliability of our subsequent findings and ensures our models are built upon a solid foundation of quality data.
